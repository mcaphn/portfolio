About Mica

Before becoming a web developer, I was a music professional for 12 years. The industry not only brought me a lot of joy and meaning, but it also taught me invaluable skills: discipline, flexibility, structure, long-term goal-setting, communication, business knowledge, patience, and an appreciation for challenge. Having these skills under my belt prepared me to become a self-taught developer.

I thrive off of creating something from nothing, and being a part of a community that’s constantly progressing and changing. My truest purpose is to produce excellent and meaningful work that will help others reach their goal.

Q&A

What resources did you use to learn?"
I took the Front-End Web Development Track at Team Treehouse. I utilized additional online resources and reinforced my practice with top-rated UX and coding books. Thanks to 22 years of studying music, I was familiar with creating practice plans, following appropriately leveled study books, and respecting the (continuing) journey rather than focusing on an end goal.

"What is unique about you?"
I've worked in many different types of businesses and organizations - retail, food service, administration, non-profit, education, live production, event coordination, and more. My experience and insight could be a great asset for an agency looking to really understand and connect with their clients.

"What kind of skills do you have that will be useful?"
I'm a self-starter. My values in education and self-development characterize me as someone who will always be keen to learn new techniques, tools, and software. I have a demonstrated ability to listen and communicate well in collaborative environments. I've been challenged enough to develop creative problem-solving skills that solve the small issues and feed the big picture.

"What do you lack in terms of skill or knowledge?"
Though I have an eye for design and a flair for art and creativity, I have no formal education in visual or graphic design. However, I am building my skills by studying and copying UI frames and websites either as UX prototypes or mock coding projects.

"What do you do for fun?"
When I’m not glued to my MacBook I can be found doing something with music, quilting, or searching for the next best thing to eat! I love food. I love learning about different cultures, have an uncontrollable collection of grey sweaters, and on Team Coco!
